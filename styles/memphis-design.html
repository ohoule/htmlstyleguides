Memphis design emerged in Milan in 1981, founded by Ettore Sottsass. The movement rejected the austere minimalism of the time, embracing bold patterns, bright colors, and asymmetrical shapes. This playful rebellion against "good taste" created a visual language that celebrates fun, irreverence, and creative freedom.

Design Principles

Embrace Chaos

Reject rigid grids and predictable layouts. Let shapes collide, overlap, and dance across the canvas with joyful abandon.

Color Fearlessly

Combine colors that "shouldn't" go together. Pink with orange, yellow with blue—there are no wrong answers, only bold choices.

Play with Scale

Make the small things big and the big things small. Unexpected proportions create visual tension and delight.

Celebrate the Strange

Good taste is the enemy of creativity. The weird, the awkward, and the unusual deserve a place at the table.
