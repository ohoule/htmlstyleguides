Design Principles

01

Depth Through Blur

Use varying blur intensities to create visual layers. Higher blur values push elements back, while sharper elements come forward.

02

Subtle Borders

Semi-transparent borders define edges without harsh lines. They catch light and add dimension to glass surfaces.

03

Colorful Backgrounds

Glass effects work best over gradient or colorful backgrounds. The blur reveals underlying colors, creating visual interest.

04

Hierarchy Matters

Use opacity levels to establish importance. More opaque elements demand attention; more transparent ones recede.
